The value is in the human moments. Writing your report, reading a peer's struggles, giving honest feedback, showing up on Wednesday. AI doesn't replace any of that.

AI removes friction today. Reminders, attendance tracking, surfacing what's ready for review. The operational layer that takes ~2 hours/week of manual work. Plus scaffolding for the hardest human moment — peer review, where 5 of 7 people currently do nothing.

The long runway: contextual intelligence. 10 moments have untapped AI potential. The biggest opportunity — surfaced by a cohort member — is industry-specific knowledge. Someone building a medical device needs different benchmarks than someone in food. AI can provide that context without writing your key results for you: regulatory timelines, unit economics, market sizing, competitive benchmarks. Every member gets a knowledgeable advisor that understands their industry, even when no peer in the room shares it.

The pattern: AI gets smarter about you over time — your industry, your stage, your patterns — and the human experience gets richer because of it. The humans do the hard, valuable work. AI makes sure they have what they need to do it well.
